## Using computer should be fun (esp. for people whose work revolves around it)!!
I have been tinkering with computers for a really, really long time now (that reminds me, need to get my knees checked). The thing that I've realized till now is that: your development/work environment matters more than you think!!! \
Working in a dingy room in a boring default-themed VS-Code editor suffocates the soul like super-duper fast. If you can lock-in in such environment then first of all, that's amazing; teach me your ways oh great one (my-socials are at the top). But, unfortunately, even if I don't like it, I'm a material guy in a material world and I want my things to be a bit cool and fancy. \
After years of trials, errors, iterations, introspection, reflections, and other hippy stuff, I think I have figured out a bunch of things that I like in my workstation (be it a personal computer, virtual instance, remote server, etc.). They are:
- Minimum information clutter in UI
- Minimum mouse use
- Couple of curated themes and color family (for editor and stuff)
- Easy, lightweight, and fast migration to different environment (like from my laptop to a remote server)
- Sidebar to the right (Important!!!)

These aren't groundbreaking discoveries as tons of people before me have figured these things out (and can relay them in more eloquent ways), this is just a way for me to convey my thoughts in these matters.
So, based on these ideas, this is tools I use daily:
- Terminal (for everything coding and knowledge-base; which basically is most of the things I use computer when I wanna be a productive member of society)
- Vim motions in as many places as possible (Mouse isn't fun to use for a long time!!)
- ... TBD

From all of these things, in this blog, I will talk about how I've been configuring my terminal to make it fun for me!
